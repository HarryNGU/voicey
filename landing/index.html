Reading, minus the strain

Hear the web in your own voice.

Voicey reads any page out loud in a voice cloned from yours, lighting up each word as it goes. For long days, tired eyes, dyslexia, or low vision, it turns reading into listening. All of it stays on your Mac.

Everything happens on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded.

Live captions

Turn any video into text, in your language.

Point Voicey at a playing video and it writes live captions of the audio. Pick English or Tiếng Việt and it translates as it listens, all on your Mac.

Private by design

Off your Mac only if you say so.

By default nothing leaves your machine. If you want your voice on a second device, turn on sync and it's locked with a key only you hold, so the file we store is one we can't open. Either way, no server ever hears what you read.

The whole point

Nothing you read leaves your Mac.

Every model runs locally, and every second of audio is made on your own machine. Your voice is personal, so it only ever leaves this Mac if you ask it to, and only locked in a way that we cannot open.
