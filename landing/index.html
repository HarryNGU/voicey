Read the web aloud in your own voice.

Voicey reads any web page out loud in a voice built from a short recording of yours, and highlights each word as it reads. It runs on your Mac. Nothing you read is sent anywhere.

Works offline. Free to use on your own Mac.

The word highlight, running now

How it works

  1. Read six short lines

    You read six sentences out loud into your Mac. It takes about two minutes. No studio and no retakes unless you want them.

  2. The voice is built on your machine

    Voicey uses that recording to build your voice locally. The audio and the finished voice stay on the computer. There is no upload step.

  3. Press play on any page

    Open an article and press play, or use the keyboard shortcut. Voicey reads it in your voice and highlights the line you are hearing.

Live captions for video

Point Voicey at a playing video and it writes down what it hears. Choose English or Tiếng Việt and it translates as it goes. The captions and the translation both run on your Mac.

Captions from a video's audio

What it does

  • Reads any pageArticles, docs, email. Or select text, right click, and read just that.
  • Highlights each wordThe word you are hearing is marked on the page, so your eyes and ears stay together.
  • Changes speedSlow it down or speed it up while it reads.
  • Keeps reading across tabsSwitch tabs and the captions follow you to whatever you are looking at.
  • Starts from the keyboardPress Alt+V on any page to start or stop.

Privacy

  • Your recordingsStay on your Mac.
  • Your voiceA file on your Mac. Delete it and it is gone.
  • Pages you readNever sent anywhere. Reading makes no network calls.
  • AccountNot needed to use it.
  • Sync to another devicePlanned, and off unless you turn it on. When on, your voice is encrypted so only you can open it.
